Introduction

Symbolic machine learning algorithms, such as those that derive programs with inductive logic programming, have the advantage of being able to learn from a small number of samples, and their synthesized solutions can often generalize to unseen problems. Additionally, these solutions are represented as readable programs, making them understandable by domain experts. On the down side, one needs to search over vast spaces of programs to synthesize such symbolic solutions, which can severely limit the use of these algorithms. Neurosymbolic programming uses neural networks and gradient descent to aid with the process of synthesizing symbolic solutions. For example, neural networks can guide the search in symbolic spaces, reduce the size of the synthesized solutions by replacing symbolic subprograms, and learn symbolic representations that are more amenable to search. Neurosymbolic programming algorithms have been applied to several problems, including reinforcement learning, supervised learning, and algorithm discovery. In this workshop, we will gather researchers from different areas to discuss recent advances in neurosymbolic programming and the future of this emerging and exciting field.

Workshop Format

This one-day workshop will consist of invited talks, show-and-tell presentations by students from the University of Alberta, a panel discussing the role of neurosymbolic programming in the future of Artificial Intelligence. The panel discussing will set the tone for small working groups, where we will discuss future research directions. We will wrap up the workshop with a short presentation from each group.

Explainable Search (Hendrik Baier)

While AI agents based on search or online planning are state of the art in many challenging domains, famously in board games, current approaches lack the ability to explain, summarize, or visualize their plans and decisions. Users struggle to understand how observable behavior is derived from considering complex spaces of possible futures and contingencies, spanned by the available actions of the agent. This limits human trust in high-stakes scenarios, as well as effective human-AI collaboration. In my talk, I will outline my vision for the research direction of explainable search, and discuss related challenges.

Program Synthesis and Deep Learning for Heuristic Search, Constraint Satisfaction and Bioacoustics

In this talk I will present a brief overview of our recent work on program synthesis in the domains of video-game related heuristic search, constraint satisfaction and bioacoustics and how neural networks come into it.
